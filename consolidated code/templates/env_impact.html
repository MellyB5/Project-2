The sheer number of animals killed during the 2019/20 Australian bushfires was devastating, with estimates upwards of 3 billion animals killed. Whilst many were killed by the heat and flames of the fires, others died as a result of starvation, dehydration and predation by feral animals. The Wildlife and Threated Species Bushfire Recovery Expert Panel has identified a provisional list of species that require urgent management intervention. Whilst many factors are taken into account when evaluating the threatened status of these animals, some key factors include their status prior to the fires and the amount of their habitat and food sources that were destroyed by the fires.
